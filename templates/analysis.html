Obesity Level Distribution
How the 6,278 individuals are classified across seven categories

Obesity Type I is the most prevalent category, affecting approximately 17% of individuals. Only 5% fall below the healthy weight threshold. The data reflects a population skewed toward overweight and obese classifications, mirroring global trends reported by the WHO.

BMI Distribution Across Population
Frequency of individuals across BMI bands

The BMI distribution is right-skewed, with a large proportion of the population falling above the healthy threshold of 24.9. The 30 to 35 band (Obesity Type I) is the most dense, suggesting a significant share of individuals have crossed into clinically significant weight territory.

Physical Activity vs Average Weight
Mean body weight at each exercise frequency level

There is a clear inverse relationship between exercise frequency and body weight. Individuals who exercise zero days per week average 87 kg, while those exercising 5 or more days average below 68 kg. This is one of the strongest predictors in the model, second only to height and weight themselves.

Diet Quality vs Obesity Rate
Obesity prevalence among high-calorie food consumers vs non-consumers

Individuals who frequently consume high-calorie foods show an obesity rate more than 2.5 times higher than those who do not. This stark difference confirms that dietary composition is a primary driver of weight classification in this dataset.

Water Intake vs Average Weight
How daily hydration correlates with body weight

Individuals consuming more than 3 litres of water daily average 16 kg less than those consuming under 1 litre. Adequate hydration supports satiety signalling and metabolic efficiency, and appears meaningfully correlated with healthier weight outcomes across this dataset.

Screen Time vs Average Weight
Sedentary technology use and its relationship to weight

Screen time above 6 hours per day is associated with an average weight 18 kg higher than those spending under 2 hours. Extended sedentary behaviour displaces physical activity and is linked to increased caloric intake during passive screen consumption.

Top Feature Importances (XGBoost)
Which variables the model weights most heavily in prediction

Body weight and height are the dominant predictors, as expected given their direct role in BMI computation. Physical activity frequency, high-calorie food consumption, and age are the top lifestyle factors. Technology usage and snacking habits round out the top eight, confirming that behaviour drives a meaningful portion of obesity risk.

Transport Mode vs Obesity Rate
Obesity prevalence by primary mode of transportation

Walking as a primary transport mode is associated with the lowest obesity prevalence at 18%, while automobile users show over 52%. This reflects cumulative daily physical activity differences as regular walkers accumulate thousands of additional steps that contribute meaningfully to energy expenditure.

Key Findings
01

Exercise is the single most impactful behaviour

After controlling for height and weight, physical activity frequency shows the strongest association with obesity classification in the model.

02

Dietary composition outweighs meal frequency

Whether individuals eat 2 or 4 meals matters far less than whether those meals contain high-calorie, ultra-processed foods.

03

Hydration is consistently undervalued

Across all obesity classifications, inadequate water intake correlates strongly with higher average weights, independent of other factors.

04

Screen time acts as a proxy for inactivity

High technology usage is a reliable signal of sedentary lifestyle, which the model identifies as a meaningful obesity risk factor.

05

Family history amplifies risk but does not determine it

Individuals with a family history of overweight show higher obesity rates, but lifestyle factors remain predictive even within this group.

06

Transport mode is a hidden activity lever

People who walk or cycle as their primary transport mode consistently appear in lower obesity categories, suggesting cumulative incidental exercise matters greatly.
